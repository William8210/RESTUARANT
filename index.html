ABOUT.

Located on a then-lonesome little corner under the Williamsburg Bridge in Brooklyn, Cascade Cafe opened its doors on New Year’s Eve twenty-two years ago. Owners Amayo William and Amayo Victory hosted and bartended and ended most nights with a hand of cards and a pitcher of margaritas. Chef Joyce Oghala and Hannah Iruvwe wrote a daily menu committed to local, seasonal, sustainable foods, and built relationships with the farmers in upstate New York and surrounding regions most especially Mrs Robinna Amayo. Blessing Imene was server and salad maker, scrawling the menu across table after table, then jumping back behind the line to overdress a salad when duty called.

Cascade cafe's expansive bar, a place to sit and read and eat and watch the world go by is the centerpiece of the room, a 90-year-old Pullman dining car. Warm wood and aged enamel, like being in the belly of a whale or the protected hold of a ship.

From the outside, Cascade Cafe might have been mistaken for a modest endeavor. But Cascade Cafe, open summer, spring, or snowstorm, has become, with the help of all the people, staff as well as guests, who continue to return to it, a room that glows from within. Over twenty years later Cascade Cafe has carried on the traditions of its early days. A place of occasion. A touchstone for a neighborhood.
